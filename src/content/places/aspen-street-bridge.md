The **Aspen Street pedestrian bridge** connects the Bozeman Creek trail corridor to **East Aspen Street**, on the public right-of-way between **Rouse Avenue** and the **Church Street Trail** (from East Cottonwood to Tamarack). The City Commission approved the project in 2011; the Northeast Urban Renewal Board later selected it as a priority for neighborhood trail access.

NENA covered the bridge in [November 2021](/news/2021-11-30-aspen-street-bridge) when the NURB selected construction and KLJ began engineering, with local architect Ken VanDeWalle contributing design work. An [April 2021 update](/news/2021-04-16-aspen-st-bridge-update) described budget trade-offs between the bridge and other NURB-funded street improvements along Front Street.

The bridge sits near [Depot Park](/places/depot-park) and the climbing boulder at the corner of East Aspen and Front streets — a key link between neighborhood streets, the creek trail, and paths farther east.
