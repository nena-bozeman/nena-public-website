Centennial Park is a 2.5-acre city park at the corner of **North Tracy Avenue** and **East Cottonwood Street** on the west side of the Northeast Neighborhood. The city set aside the land in 1934; after a housing complex was removed in the early 1980s, the park was developed with major improvements in 1983 — including a sledding berm, tree planting, and irrigation.

The park remains a neighborhood gathering spot. NENA events often meet at the **southeast corner** (N Tracy and E Cottonwood), including [NCOD consultant neighborhood walks](/events/2026-04-14-ncod-consultants-neighborhood-walk). In [December 2025](/news/2025-12-01-centennial-park-neighbors-udc-support), neighbors organized around a proposed zoning map amendment for the Centennial Park area during the city's Unified Development Code update.

See the [City of Bozeman park listing](https://www.bozeman.net/Home/Components/FacilityDirectory/FacilityDirectory/199/1863) for hours and amenities.
