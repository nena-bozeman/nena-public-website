**Northern Pacific / Story Mill Historic District**

Listed on May 10, 1996, this district is roughly bounded by the Northern Pacific Railroad right-of-way and the Story Mill spur line from Wye to Bridger Canyon Road. It reflects the industry, agriculture, and railroad technology that sustained Bozeman's growth from 1882 to 1945.

Nelson Story built a water-powered flour mill here in 1882, anticipating the railroad's arrival; the Story Mill became the Northern Pacific's first local customer in 1883 and, under his son T. Byron Story, one of the region's largest employers by the early 1900s. After an 1901 fire, a new brick mill reopened in 1904 as the Bozeman Milling Company. The district's mills, depots, livestock facilities, and related structures document three generations of the Story family's economic legacy on the northeast side of town.
