In November 1883, Bozeman's **East Side School** opened on the northeast corner of **East Mendenhall** and **Church Street**, serving families on the growing east side of town. The building was the city's second brick schoolhouse and, according to a [Gallatin County school history](https://montanagenealogy.com/gallatin/first_schools.htm), was later known as **Hawthorne School**. The same block has housed elementary classes ever since; today's [Hawthorne Elementary](https://ha.bsd7.org/) occupies the site in the [1939 Art Deco building](/history/1939-hawthorne-elementary-school-rebuilt) at **405 East Mendenhall Street**, on NENA's southern boundary beside [Beall Park](/history/1902-beall-park).

## How the school was built

As Bozeman expanded after the [Northern Pacific platting](/history/1883-founding) of the northeast, residents pressed for a school east of Main Street. According to [Bozeman Magazine's history of the site](https://bozemanmagazine.com/articles/2024/09/01/122065-the-history-of-hawthorne-east-side-school-to-art), voters backed bonds in 1882; in July 1883 **Nelson Story** and **E.W. Carpenter** purchased $10,000 in bonds to finance construction. Trustees bought ten lots from **Charles Rich**—fronting Mendenhall and Church—for $1,250.

Architect **Byron Vreeland** (who also designed Bozeman's City Hall and Opera House) drew the plans; builder **August Zietzke** completed the two-story brick school for $11,900. The school opened in November 1883 with **Mrs. A.G. Smith** and **Miss Cora E. Bates** as its first teachers, serving grades one through eight. Enrollment grew quickly; by 1887 roughly seventy students attended, and neighbors soon raised money for water on the grounds and the planting of shade trees. A bell tower followed in 1891 to signal the start of classes.

In **1905**, when a third east-side elementary school opened, Bozeman renamed its schools for authors: the East Side School became **Hawthorne**, alongside Irving and Longfellow. The brick building with the bell tower served the neighborhood for more than half a century before it was replaced by Fred Willson's [1939 Moderne school](/history/1939-hawthorne-elementary-school-rebuilt) on the same corner.
