In **November 1939**, Bozeman dedicated a new **Hawthorne School** on **East Mendenhall Street**, replacing the brick [East Side School](/history/1883-hawthorne-elementary-east-side-school-opens) that had stood on the same block since 1883. The current [Hawthorne Elementary](https://ha.bsd7.org/) building—at **405 East Mendenhall**, on NENA's southern boundary beside [Beall Park](/history/1902-beall-park)—dates from this Depression-era rebuild.

## PWA modernization

Locals launched Bozeman's most ambitious school construction program in **June 1938**, when voters approved bonds for new elementary schools and an addition to Emerson Junior High despite the lingering effects of the Great Depression. Federal **Public Works Administration** aid covered roughly forty-five percent of the cost. Architect **Fred Willson** designed a single **Moderne** plan for **Hawthorne**, Irving, and Longfellow schools—reinforced concrete, horizontal façades, glass block, nine classrooms, gymnasium-auditoriums, and project rooms for hands-on learning. Workers finished all three elementary buildings within about fifteen months, as summarized on [Historic Montana's Longfellow School entry](https://historicmt.org/items/show/2222) (Longfellow shares the same design).

Construction on Hawthorne began in **1938** and wrapped in November 1939. According to [Bozeman Magazine's history of the school](https://bozemanmagazine.com/articles/2024/09/01/122065-the-history-of-hawthorne-east-side-school-to-art), the formal dedication was held **December 1, 1939**; the *Bozeman Courier* praised the result as among the most modern grade-school buildings in the country. The Art Deco–influenced structure sits at the prominent corner of **Rouse** and **Mendenhall**, visible to anyone heading downtown from the north side.

## Legacy on the northeast side

The 1939 building kept Hawthorne on the east side's main residential corridor after the neighborhood had known the school for more than fifty years under the names East Side School (1883) and Hawthorne (from **1905**, when Bozeman's three elementary schools were named for authors). Together with Emerson Junior High and the institutions around [Beall Park](/history/1902-beall-park), it has anchored public education in the northeast for generations. Hawthorne became a model school for arts integration in **1991** and received a classroom addition in **2017**, but the 1939 Willson design remains the face of the campus.
