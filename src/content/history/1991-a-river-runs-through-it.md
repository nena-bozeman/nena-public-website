In the summer of 1991, Robert Redford brought a major Hollywood production to southwest Montana. *A River Runs Through It*—his adaptation of Norman Maclean's novella—was shot from June 17 through August 31, 1991, with crews working around Livingston, the Gallatin River canyon south of Bozeman, and several locations in Bozeman itself. The film premiered in 1992 and won the Academy Award for Best Cinematography.

## Bozeman locations

Although the story is set in Missoula, much of the production used Montana communities east of the Continental Divide. In Bozeman, the trackside exterior of the **Northern Pacific passenger depot** at 820 Front Street stood in for Missoula's railroad station. Police-station scenes were filmed at **32 South Tracy Avenue**, at the edge of the South Tracy Avenue Historic District. The [**Misco Mill**](/history/1933-misco-grain-elevator-built-on-wallace-avenue)—the historic MISCO grain elevator at **700 North Wallace Avenue**—served as the gambling house frequented by Paul Maclean (Brad Pitt). Both the depot and the mill sit within the Northeast Neighborhood's historic core along the railroad corridor.

![Former Northern Pacific passenger depot at 820 Front Street in Bozeman, a filming location for A River Runs Through It in 1991.](/images/history/bozeman-depot-south-faces.jpg)

Redford told a local interviewer that he "loved being here" and that Montana's people and landscapes were essential to finishing the film on an ambitious schedule. Fly-fishing sequences were shot on the **Gallatin**, **Boulder**, and **West Boulder** rivers; street and church scenes were largely filmed in Livingston.

## Legacy in the neighborhood

The depot remains a landmark along Front Street, and the film is still closely associated with Bozeman's identity as a river town. Montana PBS later documented the production in [*Shadow Casting: The Making of "A River Runs Through It"*](https://watch.montanapbs.org/video/shadow-casting-the-making-of-a-river-runs-through-it-pd7adb/), and the [Montana Film Office](https://montanafilm.com/) maintains a gallery of behind-the-scenes images from the shoot.
