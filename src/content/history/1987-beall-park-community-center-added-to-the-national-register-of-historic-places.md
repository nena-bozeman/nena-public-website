**Beall Park Community Center**

The Beall Park Community Center at 409 North Bozeman Avenue was listed on the National Register on October 23, 1987 as part of the Bozeman Multiple Resource Area. Built in 1927 and designed by W.R. Plew, a civil engineer and professor of architecture at Montana State College, the fieldstone and cobblestone building is an excellent example of Craftsman architecture and Plew's most significant work.

Philanthropist Ella T. Martin donated the building to the city for use as a community recreation center. It later housed the Bozeman Nursery School (c. 1955–1983) and the Beall Park Art Center (1983–2007). The structure stands at the heart of Beall Park, which has served the northeast neighborhood as a gathering place for over a century.
